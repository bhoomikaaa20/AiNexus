What is Computer Vision?

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that enables computers and systems to derive meaningful information from digital images, videos, and other visual inputs — and take actions or make recommendations based on that information. If AI enables computers to think, computer vision enables them to see, observe and understand.

Key Capabilities

  • Image Classification: Identifying what broad category an image belongs to (e.g., determining if a photo contains a dog).
  • Object Detection: Identifying specific objects within an image and locating their exact boundaries.
  • Facial Recognition: Identifying or verifying a person from a digital image or a video frame.

Today, computer vision is widely used in facial recognition systems to unlock smartphones, in self-driving cars to detect pedestrians and street signs, and in medical imaging to spot anomalies like tumors in X-rays.
