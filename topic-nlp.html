What is NLP?

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that helps computers understand, interpret, and manipulate human language. NLP draws from many disciplines, including computer science and computational linguistics, in its pursuit to fill the gap between human communication and computer understanding.

Common NLP Tasks

  • Speech Recognition: Converting spoken language into text (e.g., Siri, Alexa).
  • Sentiment Analysis: Identifying the mood or subjective opinions within large amounts of text (e.g., tracking brand reputation on Twitter).
  • Machine Translation: Automatically translating text from one human language to another (e.g., Google Translate).
  • Chatbots: Conversational agents that can answer questions or perform tasks via text or voice.

Modern NLP relies heavily on deep learning algorithms, particularly Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT, which are trained on vast amounts of internet text to generate remarkably human-like responses.
